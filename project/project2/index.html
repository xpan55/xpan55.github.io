Effects of Linguistic Labels on Object Categorization and Perception.

Investigated the impact of linguistic label structure on cognitive categorization and perception.
bilingual label feedback ERP oddball paradigm

This project has been published on Bilingualism: Language and Cognition amd Language and Cognition:

Pan, X., & Jared, D. (2021). Effects of Chinese word structure on object perception in Chinese- English bilinguals: Evidence from an ERP visual oddball paradigm. Bilingualism: Language and Cognition, 24(1), 111–123.

Pan, X., & Jared, D. (2020). Effects of Chinese word structure on object categorization in Chinese-English bilinguals. Language and Cognition, 12(3), 468–500.

and has been presented at Canadian Society for Brain, Behaviour and Cognitive Science 2019 annual meeting, Waterloo, Canada, and the 11th International Conference on The Mental Lexicon, Edmonton, Canada:

Talk:

Pan, X., & Jared, D. (2019, June). The effects of language on object perception: Evidence from a visual oddball task with ERP. Canadian Society for Brain, Behaviour and Cognitive Science, Waterloo, Canada.

Poster:

Pan, X., & Jared, D. (2018, September). The effects of language on categorization and perception. 11th International Conference on The Mental Lexicon, Edmonton, Canada.
